Pick the mode that matches **who should read the message**. Wrong mode is the easiest way to get hurt.

## Everyone nearby

**Who reads it:** Anyone in Bluetooth range with the app — **including police running this app**.

**Use for:** Crowd warnings only (“exit blocked at gate 4”).

<Aside type="danger" title="In-app warning">
ANYONE nearby can read this, including police running this app. Never put names, plans or locations here.
</Aside>

## Channel

**Who reads it:** Anyone who knows the passphrase — including people who join later (they can read old messages too).

**Use for:** A loose group that grows by word of mouth.

There is **no owner, no admin, no kick**. A leaked passphrase ends the channel. Start a new one.

<Aside type="caution" title="In-app warning">
Anyone who knows this channel’s passphrase can read everything in it, including messages sent before they joined.
</Aside>

## Group

**Who reads it:** Only the people you personally added (up to 15).

**Use for:** Your actual crew.

Each message is sealed separately to each member. Your phone’s member list is **local** — it can disagree with someone else’s list if you add people differently.

## Direct

**Who reads it:** One person — after you add them and preferably **verify** in person.

**Use for:** Anything sensitive.

<Aside type="caution" title="Until verified">
Not verified yet — anyone could be on the other end. Check the safety number together, in person.
</Aside>

When verified, the app treats the chat as encrypted to that person only. Verification does not replace good judgment about what you write.

The app cannot make you safe by itself. These habits matter as much as the crypto.

## Do

- **Meet in person** to add and verify contacts. Compare safety numbers on both screens before you trust a chat.
- **Keep the app open** when you need to send, receive, or help relay for others. Pocketed phones often stop helping the mesh.
- **Choose the mode on purpose.** Sensitive words go in **direct** (verified) or a small **group** — never in Everyone nearby.
- **Treat channel passphrases like secrets.** If one leaks, abandon the channel.
- **Use panic wipe** if the phone may be taken: Settings → Panic wipe. Know that a seized unlocked phone can still be dangerous; wipe is not magic.
- **Prefer a build you trust.** Compile it yourself, or take it from a source you have reason to trust.

## Don’t

- Don’t put names, routes, safe houses, or plans in **Everyone nearby**.
- Don’t verify someone over a screenshot or a video call you cannot trust — verify standing together.
- Don’t assume “encrypted” means “safe to say anything.” Metadata, presence, and mistakes still exist.
- Don’t expect the app to work with the screen off or after days unused without checking.
- Don’t share your panic outcome as “fully erased” if wipe reported a failure — read the error.

## Panic wipe

Panic wipe destroys local identity material and message history, then starts fresh when it succeeds. If a step fails, the app should say so — treat that as **not clean**.

<Aside type="caution">
After a wipe, the radio may come back under a new identity. Move location if that matters for you.
</Aside>

## Your duty of care to others

If you relay for strangers (any phone running the app can), you are part of the network. Don’t run experiments that flood the mesh at a real gathering. Don’t encourage people to depend on this as their only channel yet.
